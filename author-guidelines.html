Journal Overview

The Journal of Technology, Engineering, Architecture and Design (JTEAD) is the official peer-reviewed research journal of the College of Technology, University of San Agustin. It publishes original research, reviews, and design-based scholarship that advance knowledge and practice across technology, engineering, architecture, and design disciplines.

Vision

To be a recognized platform for scholarly research that bridges technology, engineering, architecture, and design in service of sustainable development and community progress.

Mission

JTEAD is committed to disseminating high-quality, ethically sound research; fostering interdisciplinary collaboration among students, faculty, and practitioners; and supporting evidence-based innovation that benefits industry, education, and society.

Future Direction

The journal aims to expand its readership and contributor base beyond the University of San Agustin, pursue indexing in recognized regional and international databases, and continue strengthening its editorial and peer-review processes in line with international publishing standards.

Types of Articles

JTEAD accepts several categories of manuscripts, each subject to the journal's editorial and peer-review standards. Authors should select the category that best matches the nature and scope of their work.

Original Research Articles

Reports of original, previously unpublished research findings following the IMRaD structure (Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion).

Review Articles

Comprehensive, critical syntheses of existing literature on a specific topic that identify trends, gaps, and directions for future research.

Technical Notes

Short reports describing a novel technique, tool, methodology, or piece of equipment relevant to the journal's scope, without necessarily presenting complete research findings.

Short Communications

Brief reports of preliminary or focused research findings that merit rapid dissemination but do not warrant a full-length article.

Case Studies

Detailed accounts of a specific project, application, or real-world problem, illustrating practical solutions relevant to engineering, technology, architecture, or design practice.

Design Research

Manuscripts documenting design-based inquiry, including design processes, prototypes, and design outputs in architecture, engineering, and related creative disciplines.

Educational Innovation Papers

Studies presenting innovative pedagogical approaches, curriculum development, or instructional technologies applied to technology, engineering, architecture, and design education.

Perspective Articles

Opinion or position papers offering informed commentary on emerging issues, trends, or challenges within the journal's fields of interest.

Letters to the Editor

Short communications responding to previously published articles, or raising issues of relevance to the journal's readership.

Publication Ethics

JTEAD adheres to the publication ethics and malpractice standards set forth by the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE). All parties involved in the publication process—authors, reviewers, and editors—are expected to uphold these standards.

Ethical Principles

The journal is committed to ensuring that all published research complies with recognized ethical standards for academic publishing, honesty, and accountability.

Responsibilities of Authors

Authors are responsible for the accuracy and integrity of their submitted work, for ensuring all co-authors approve the final manuscript, and for disclosing any conflicts of interest or funding sources.

Authorship

Authorship should be limited to individuals who have made a significant contribution to the conception, design, execution, or interpretation of the study, and who agree to be accountable for its content.

Originality and Plagiarism

Submitted manuscripts must be original work. Authors must ensure that they have written entirely original works, and that the work or words of others have been appropriately cited or quoted.

Duplicate Submission and Redundant Publication

Manuscripts submitted to JTEAD must not be under consideration elsewhere, and must not substantially duplicate previously published work.

Data Integrity

Authors must present an accurate account of the research performed and provide sufficient detail and raw data, where requested, to allow for verification of results.

Image Integrity

Figures and images must accurately represent the original data. Any adjustment to brightness, contrast, or color balance must be applied uniformly and disclosed where it affects interpretation.

Artificial Intelligence

The use of AI and AI-assisted technologies in the writing process must be disclosed in the manuscript. AI tools cannot be listed as authors and do not bear responsibility for the content of the manuscript.

Human Participants and Animal Research

Research involving human participants or animals must comply with relevant ethical guidelines and institutional approvals, and the manuscript must state that informed consent or ethical clearance was obtained.

Indigenous Knowledge and Community Engagement

Research involving indigenous communities or traditional knowledge must demonstrate appropriate community consultation, consent, and respect for cultural protocols.

Conflict of Interest

All authors, reviewers, and editors must disclose any financial, personal, or professional relationships that could be perceived as influencing the objectivity of the research or the review process.

Reviewer Responsibilities

Reviewers are expected to provide objective, constructive, and timely evaluations, maintain confidentiality of the manuscript, and disclose any conflicts of interest that may affect their review.

Editorial Responsibilities

Editors are responsible for making fair, unbiased decisions on manuscripts based on their scholarly merit, free from commercial or personal considerations.

Corrections and Retractions

The journal will issue corrections, clarifications, or retractions as necessary when errors, misconduct, or ethical violations are identified in published work.

Appeals and Complaints

Authors who wish to appeal an editorial decision or raise a complaint regarding the review process may do so in writing to the Editorial Office, which will investigate the matter following the journal's established procedures.

Publication Ethics Workflow

Ethical concerns raised at any stage of submission or after publication are investigated by the Editorial Board following a defined workflow of screening, inquiry, and resolution.

Ethics Checklist for Authors

Before submission, authors should confirm originality, disclose conflicts of interest, secure necessary ethics approvals, and ensure all co-authors have consented to the submission.

Preparing Your Manuscript

This section outlines the formatting, structural, and technical requirements authors must follow when preparing a manuscript for submission to JTEAD.

General Requirements

Manuscripts must be typed in a legible font (e.g., Times New Roman, 12pt), double-spaced, with 1-inch margins on all sides, and include continuous line and page numbers.

Language

Manuscripts must be written in clear, formal academic English. Authors who are non-native English speakers are encouraged to have their manuscript proofread prior to submission.

File Format and Title Page Requirements

Manuscripts must adhere to the following file, structural, and title page requirements before they can be accepted for review.

File Format

Manuscripts should be submitted as an editable Microsoft Word (.doc or .docx) file.

Accepted File Formats

Accepted file formats include .docx, .doc, and .pdf for the main manuscript; figures should be submitted separately in .jpg, .png, or .tiff format at a minimum of 300 dpi.

Manuscript structure

The manuscript should follow a logical structure consisting of a title page, abstract, keywords, main body, and references, as detailed in the sections below.

Title Page Requirements

The title page must be submitted as a separate section and include the article title, author names, affiliations, and corresponding author details.

Article Title

The title should be concise, specific, and informative, accurately reflecting the content and scope of the study.

Author Information

Full names, institutional affiliations, and ORCID iDs (if available) must be provided for each author.

Corresponding Author

One author must be designated as the corresponding author and provide a complete mailing address, email, and contact number for editorial correspondence.

Manuscript Structure

The main body of the manuscript should generally follow the IMRaD format: Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion, followed by a Conclusion and References.

Abstract

The abstract should be a single paragraph of 200–300 words summarizing the objective, methodology, key findings, and significance of the study.

Keywords

Authors should provide 4–6 keywords that reflect the main topics of the manuscript to aid indexing and searchability.

Keywords should be separated by commas, should not repeat words already used in the title, and should use standard terms commonly found in the field.

Introduction

The Introduction should place the study in context, explain the problem being addressed, and state the research objectives clearly. It should give readers enough background to understand the significance of the work.

Materials and Methods

The Materials and Methods section should describe experimental design, data collection, materials, tools, and procedures in sufficient detail to allow replication. Include any software, equipment, or analytical approaches used.

Results

The Results section should present findings clearly and objectively, using tables, figures, and descriptive text as needed. Avoid interpretation in this section; reserve that for Discussion.

Discussion

The Discussion should interpret the results, compare them with existing literature, explain their significance, and discuss limitations and implications for future research.

Conclusions

Conclusions should summarize the main findings and highlight the practical or theoretical contributions of the study. Keep this section concise and focused on the core messages.

Acknowledgements

The Acknowledgements section may recognize funding sources, institutional support, and individuals who contributed to the work but do not meet authorship criteria.

Funding Statement

If applicable, include a Funding Statement that lists the financial support received for the work, including grant numbers and funding agencies.

Author Contributions

Provide a brief description of each author's contribution to the research, manuscript preparation, and submission process.

Conflict of Interest

Authors must disclose any financial or personal relationships that could influence the work. If none exist, include a statement that the authors have no conflicts of interest.

Data Availability Statement

Include a statement describing whether and how the data supporting the findings are available, including any access restrictions.

References Within Text

Cite all sources used in the manuscript consistently and accurately. Follow the journal's reference style for in-text citations and bibliography entries.

Reference Verification and Linking

Ensure that all references are correct, complete, and verifiable. Include DOIs or URLs where available, and check that all cited works are actually referenced in the text.

Web References

Web references should include the author or organization, title, website name, URL, and date accessed.

Data References

Data references should cite the dataset source, repository or archive, DOI, and any relevant accession numbers.

Preprint References

Preprints may be cited if they are relevant, but authors should clearly indicate their preprint status and include the source or DOI.

Reference Management Software

Authors may use reference management software to format citations, but should verify the final reference list for consistency and accuracy.

Appendices

Appendices may be used for supplementary material that is important but not essential to the main text, such as detailed data tables or additional figures.

Manuscript Preparation Checklist

Use a checklist to confirm that the manuscript includes all required sections, follows formatting guidelines, and contains accurate references, figures, tables, and author information.

Artwork, Figures, Tables, Equations, References, and Supplementary Materials

This section explains how to prepare and submit artwork, figures, tables, equations, and supplementary files for review and publication.

Introduction

Artwork should be clear, legible, and suitable for both print and digital presentation. Follow the journal's guidance on resolution, size, and format.

Artwork and Figures

Submit figures as separate high-resolution files and include descriptive captions. Ensure that all images are appropriately labeled and referenced in the text.

Engineering Drawings and Design Outputs

Engineering drawings and design outputs should be submitted at a resolution that preserves detail and clarity. Provide scale, dimensions, and notation as needed.

Color and Accessibility

Use color thoughtfully and ensure that figures remain understandable when printed in grayscale. Avoid color combinations that are inaccessible to color-blind readers.

Tables

Tables should be numbered consecutively and include clear headings. Place tables close to the first text reference and keep them concise.

Mathematical Equations

Number equations sequentially and format them consistently. Define all symbols and abbreviations used in equations.

Units, Symbols, and Nomenclature

Use standard units and symbols consistently throughout the manuscript. Define any specialized or uncommon notation.

Supplementary Materials

Supplementary materials may include datasets, videos, code, or additional figures. Submit these files separately and provide a description in the manuscript.

Graphical Abstract

If a graphical abstract is provided, it should summarize the key message of the paper visually and be suitable for online display.

Research Highlights

Research highlights are brief bullet points that summarize the main contributions and significance of the work.

Submission Quality Checklist

Before submission, verify that the manuscript meets the journal's formatting and content requirements, including author information, references, figures, and ethical statements.

Submission Process

This section outlines the steps authors should expect from manuscript submission through editorial review and publication decisions.

Pre-submission Requirements

Ensure the manuscript is complete, complies with formatting rules, and includes all required supporting documents before submission.

Required Submission Files

Required files typically include the main manuscript, title page, abstract, figures, tables, supplementary materials, and a cover letter.

Cover Letter

The cover letter should introduce the manuscript, explain its significance, and confirm that the work is original and not under consideration elsewhere.

Editorial Office Screening

The editorial office performs an initial screening for scope, completeness, formatting, and compliance with ethical standards.

Initial Editorial Assessment

The Editor-in-Chief or assigned editor evaluates the manuscript's suitability for review and may reject or send it for peer review.

Reviewer Selection

Appropriate reviewers are selected based on expertise, and authors may be asked to suggest potential reviewers or identify those with conflicts of interest.

Revision Process

Authors may receive reviewer comments and requested revisions. Revised manuscripts should include a response letter describing how each comment was addressed.

Acceptance

If accepted, the manuscript proceeds to production and publication, subject to final editorial approval and any required formatting adjustments.

Withdrawal of Manuscripts

Authors may request withdrawal prior to acceptance, but should do so only for valid reasons and notify the editorial office promptly.

Author Responsibilities

Authors are responsible for the accuracy of their work, disclosure of conflicts, proper authorship attribution, and adherence to journal policies.
