Review Process

The Journal of Technology, Engineering, Architecture, and Design (JTEAD) employs a double-blind peer review process to ensure the quality, integrity, and credibility of published research. Each submitted manuscript is evaluated by experts in the relevant field who provide constructive and confidential reviews.

Overview of the Review Process

1. Submission

The author submits the manuscript through the online submission system.

2. Initial Assessment

The Editorial Office conducts an initial assessment for scope, quality, and compliance.

3. Reviewer Selection

The Section Editor selects at least two independent reviewers with relevant expertise.

4. Peer Review

Reviewers evaluate the manuscript and submit their confidential reviews.

5. Editorial Decision

The Section Editor recommends a decision based on reviewer comments.

6. Revision

The author revises the manuscript and submits a response to reviewers.

Types of Editorial Decisions

Accept

The manuscript is accepted without further revision.

Minor Revision

Minor changes are required before the manuscript can be accepted.

Major Revision

Substantial revisions are required. The manuscript may be sent for another round of review.

Reject

The manuscript is not suitable for publication in the journal.

Reject and Resubmit

The author may submit a substantially revised manuscript as a new submission.

Review Policy

All reviews are conducted confidentially under a double-blind process, meaning the identities of both authors and reviewers are concealed from each other. Reviewers are expected to provide objective, constructive, and timely evaluations.

Review Timeline

The review process usually takes 3–5 weeks, depending on the availability of reviewers and the complexity of the manuscript.

The Editorial Office will keep authors informed at every stage of the review process via email. Authors are encouraged to check their registered email regularly.
