Aims and scope

The Journal of Technology, Engineering, Architecture, and Design publishes original, peer-reviewed research and scholarly works that advance knowledge, practice, and innovation in engineering, technology, architecture, design, and creative disciplines. It serves as a multidisciplinary platform for students, faculty, researchers, and practitioners of the College of Technology, University of San Agustin to share research outputs, applied studies, technical developments, and design-based investigations. The journal welcomes works that address scientific, technological, environmental, industrial, creative, and societal challenges. Emphasis is placed on studies that demonstrate originality, academic rigor, practical relevance, sustainability, interdisciplinary collaboration, and community impact. Through scholarly publication, the journal supports research excellence, innovation, and knowledge exchange within and beyond the university.

Particular areas of interest are, but are not limited to:

Chemical Engineering:

process engineering, reaction engineering, separation processes, transport phenomena, catalysis, energy systems, process safety, biochemical engineering, environmental engineering, waste valorization, and sustainable manufacturing.

Civil Engineering:

structural engineering, geotechnical engineering, transportation systems, construction management, water resources, disaster resilience, smart infrastructure, environmental engineering, and sustainable construction materials.

Mechanical Engineering:

machine design, manufacturing systems, thermal sciences, renewable energy, robotics, automation, mechatronics, fluid mechanics, HVAC, and industrial applications.

Electronics and Computer Engineering:

embedded systems, artificial intelligence, Internet of Things (IoT), communications, control systems, cybersecurity, software engineering, data analytics, machine learning, computer vision, and digital technologies.

Architecture and Built Environment:

architectural design, sustainable architecture, urban planning, heritage conservation, smart cities, building technologies, digital fabrication, Building Information Modeling (BIM), and resilient communities.

Landscape Architecture:

landscape planning and design, ecological restoration, green infrastructure, environmental planning, public open spaces, urban ecology, climate adaptation, and sustainable landscapes.

Interior Design:

interior architecture, space planning, furniture design, human-centered design, adaptive reuse, lighting, materials, digital visualization, and sustainable interiors.

Fine Arts and Creative Design:

visual arts, digital media, graphic communication, creative technologies, product design, cultural heritage, interdisciplinary art, and design innovation.

Interdisciplinary Research:

studies integrating engineering, architecture, design, computing, environmental sustainability, smart technologies, education, entrepreneurship, and community development.
